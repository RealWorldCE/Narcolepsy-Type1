Section 1

Orexin-Based Pathophysiology

psychology The Knowledge Gap

The Role of Orexin

NT1 is now recognized as a highly selective, autoimmune-mediated destruction of the orexin-producing neurons in the lateral hypothalamus. Only 23.1% of physicians in a recent survey correctly identified low levels of orexin as characteristic of NT1.

Genetic Factor: The primary genetic risk factor is the HLA-DQB1*06:02 allele, present in over 98% of NT1 cases, allowing presentation of orexin-derived peptides to T-cells.

The "Flip-Flop" Switch & Receptors

hub

Loss of Tone

Orexin exerts a stabilizing influence on wakefulness. The absence of orexin-mediated excitatory tone permits inappropriate REM intrusion into the awake state (cataplexy).

sensors

OX2R Targeting

While OX1R binds orexin-A, OX2R plays a uniquely critical role in maintaining wakefulness via Gq/PLC/IP3/Ca2+ signaling. It is the primary target for emerging therapies.

Section 2

Objective Diagnostic Criteria

Symptom Identification

Although 96% of physicians feel comfortable diagnosing narcolepsy, a recent survey found only 4% could correctly identify all five classic symptoms.

checklist

The CHESS Symptoms

Cataplexy, Hallucinations, EDS, Sleep paralysis, Sleep disruption.

rule

Screening Tools

The Swiss Narcolepsy Scale (SNS) shows 98% specificity for NT1, significantly outperforming the widely used ESS.

ICSD-3-TR Diagnostic Criteria for NT1

Patients must have Excessive Daytime Sleepiness (EDS) for at least 3 months, PLUS one or both of the following objective findings:

science Biomarker Pathway

Low or absent CSF hypocretin-1 (orexin-A) levels measured by radioimmunoassay ≤ 110 pg/mL.

bedtime Sleep Study Pathway

Cataplexy AND an MSLT showing mean sleep latency ≤ 8 minutes with ≥ 2 SOREMPs.

Section 3

Emerging Orexin-Based Therapies

medication Oveporexton (TAK-861)

FDA Priority Review granted (Action expected Q3 2026).

  • check_circle Mean change from baseline sleep latency up to +25.4 minutes.
  • check_circle 80% median reduction in weekly cataplexy rates.
  • check_circle Mild TEAEs: insomnia, urinary urgency/frequency.

medication Alixorexton (ALKS 2680)

FDA Breakthrough Designation (Phase 3 expected Q1 2026).

  • check_circle Achieved clinically normative wakefulness (up to 28 mins on MWT).
  • check_circle Dose-dependent improvements with no serious TEAEs.

The Paradigm Shift

Historically, treatments (stimulants, modafinil, oxybates) targeted symptoms rather than neurobiology. The new class of selective OX2R agonists directly substitute for the deficient orexin, stimulating the necessary Gq/PLC/Ca2+ pathways.

Early Pipeline Watch

  • ORX750 (Centessa): Phase 2 success showing >20m increase in sleep latency.
  • E2086 (Eisai): Phase 2 data indicating higher alertness vs modafinil.
  • JZP-441 (Jazz): Recruiting for Phase 1/2 trials.
